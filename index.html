Understanding BMI Categories
🔵
Below 18.5 — Underweight
May indicate nutritional deficiency. Focus on calorie-dense whole foods and resistance training.
🟢
18.5 – 24.9 — Normal weight
Associated with the lowest health risks. Maintain through balanced diet and regular exercise.
🟡
25 – 29.9 — Overweight
Elevated risk of metabolic conditions. A small calorie deficit and increased movement can help.
🔴
30 and above — Obese
Higher risk of serious health conditions. Consult a healthcare professional for a personalised plan.
⚠️ BMI does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or body composition. Athletes may have a high BMI without excess fat. Use it as one indicator among many.
